About Me

I work at the intersection of kidney health, research, and community advocacy. My days are spent pushing for equity in transplant access, supporting patients, and helping reshape systems that have failed Black and Brown communities for generations. My lived experience as a kidney transplant recipient guides the heart of my work.

My coding journey started because I needed better tools to serve people. I realized that the websites, platforms, and digital systems meant to support patients were outdated, confusing, and built without our communities in mind. Coding gives me the ability to create accessible resources, improve patient education, and design platforms that honor the communities I fight for every day.
